Parchment Echo

For best experience, project this video and turn the volume all the way up
The 'obliteration' of an object, the decomposition and deconstruction of it's systemic and cultural canons. What did this manifest as for paper? An invention that I believe would have materialized in every timeline of human existance. It's effects on the world incomprehensable and it's origins sylvan. If your birth certificate, passport, ID, work permit, study permit, diplomas, drivers license, social security card, heath insurance, will, Marriage certificate ceased to exist. What are you then? You are certainly not any of the things listed. You aren't eligible to travel, eligible for healthcare, married, educated or born. What are we if not government documents. In "Seeing like a state", James C. Scott, writes about the significance of paper documentation. How the state relies on standardizing and making our lives legible so that we are easier to govern and dominate. In the process, nuance of our lives are lost. We are conditioned to live within the constraints of what the government has written down for us. Parchment Echo is an experimental film inspired by Issac Goes. It serves as a dialouge between my identity as a Pakistani immigrant unfixed between Dubai and Canada. The film consistes of found footage from WWI, WWII, The partition of Hindustan and my own found footage from past moments in my life. Most of the footage is not directly imported from a camera. Instead, it is scanned or turned into stop motion mixed media as a constraint to work with the medium of paper internally as well as externally. The dialouge in urdu was recorded by my mother where she talks about meeting her nana (het late mother's brother) who got permission from the Indian government to travel to Pakistan in 2023, along with a voiceover of me reading out a summary of my finding from "Seeing like a state".
